Shyvana is a fearsome half-dragon warrior. Though she often appears humanoid, she also rules the skies as a dragon, incinerating her foes with fiery breath. Having saved the life of the crown prince Jarvan IV, Shyvana now serves uneasily in his royal guard, struggling to find acceptance among the suspicious people of Demacia.

Background

[edit | edit source]

Early Life

[edit | edit source]

Shyvana was born to Yvva, a fiercely territorial elemental dragon who lived beneath a volcano, where she would guard her eggs and feast on any mortals foolish enough to steal them. One such egg was stolen by a mage, and not long after he had fled, it hatched. But instead of a baby dragon, the hatchling took the form of a humanoid baby girl with pale violet skin. The mage grew fond of the little girl and decided to raise her as his daughter, naming her "Shyvana" after the dark legend of her broodmother.[1]

As Shyvana grew, she discovered more of her latent abilities, which allowed her to transform between her human form and that of a dragon at will. Though her unique nature left her isolated from the people of Valoran, Shyvana embraced her new abilities and began to hunt prey in the wilderness. During one of her excursions, however, Shyvana stumbled upon Yvva's territory, and the elder beast gave chase, not even caring that Shyvana was her own daughter. Though Shyvana managed to escape, she returned home to find that Yvva was there, and she could only watch as her adoptive father was killed by the dragon's fire.[1]

Following Yvva's attack, Shyvana brought her father's body into the forest. As there was nothing she could do to save him, Shyvana gave him a simple burial before wandering alone into the rest of the forests.[1]

Encounter with Quinn

[edit | edit source]
Main article: Salvation

After several months hiding in the wilderness, Shyvana tracked the scent of her mother's fire to a subterranean settlement; Yvva was burning the entire town. A Demacian scout named Quinn and her azurite eagle Valor were the settlement's only defense,[1] and unwilling to watch innocent people be killed, Shyvana assumed her draconic form and flew to their rescue. Although Shyvana was physically overpowered by Yvva, Quinn and Valor were able to support her long enough for the townspeople to escape. The three of them then flew to safety as the cavern collapsed, blocking Yvva's pursuit. Quinn brought the exhausted Shyvana away from the scene,[2] and having seen Quinn's courage in the battle, Shyvana regarded her with respect before they parted ways.[1]

Stand at Castle Wrenwall

[edit | edit source]
“ "Fury. The only thing you gave me, mother. A fire that destroys. But courage, purpose... I found those on my own."
— Shyvana, Forged in Fury

Following the battle in the subterranean village, Shyvana roamed the forests on high alert, aware that Yvva might track her down for vengeance at any moment.[1] Instead of her mother, however, she found a wounded warrior in the forests near the Argent Mountains. The man was on the brink of death after fleeing from Noxian soldiers, and as he lost consciousness, he mistook her silhouette for that of the legendary Winged Protector. After frightening off his pursuers,[3] Shyvana carried the man to the Demacian border settlement of Wrenwall, without even knowing who he was.[1] At the gates, the soldiers were initially hostile to Shyvana's scaly appearance, so to ensure she would be welcomed there, the man entrusted her with the crest of his family, the royal house of Lightshield.[4] It was also here that Shyvana learned that this man was Jarvan IV, the crown prince of Demacia.[1]

As Jarvan recovered, Shyvana watched over him, and he judged her a kind and worthy companion despite the suspicions of the other Demacians. But the brief peace would not last as Shyvana felt Yvva's approach on the horizon, and the soldiers began to ready the defense of Wrenwall and the townspeople. Still recovering from his injuries, Jarvan went to marshal the garrison, and Shyvana decided that her best option was to flee back into the wilderness to lure Yvva away from Wrenwall. As she was leaving, Jarvan caught Shyvana and confronted her, after which she confessed to everything—the great beast on the horizon was her mother, and she could not allow innocent people to die because of her. However, Jarvan refused to let her leave; Shyvana had saved his life, and to repay her kindness, he and the Demacians would stand with her and end the beast once and for all.[1][5]

Jarvan and Shyvana proved a deadly team during the battle. The power of her transformation allowed her take the skies, and his leadership of the garrison, its archers, and healers held the line to support her. In the end they were victorious, and as thanks, Jarvan offered Shyvana a place in his personal guard. They returned to the Great City of Demacia together and presented the dragon's skull to King Jarvan III in the Hall of Valor.[1] Jarvan's father was overjoyed to learn his son was alive, but the nobles of his court regarded Shyvana with suspicion. Even so, Jarvan made certain she would have a place in Demacia, along with those who fought alongside them at Wrenwall.[5]

Encounter with the Vellox

[edit | edit source]
Main article: The Winged Beast

One day while in the service of Demacia, Shyvana was patrolling the northern hills of the kingdom when she smelled human blood and followed the trail to the gated watchtower. The guard on lookout, Thomme, was being eaten by a vellox, who offered the corpse to Shyvana and fled upon seeing her. Shyvana assumed her dragon form and pursued the vellox so should could avenge her fellow soldier, tearing through and burning large swaths of the forest until her prey was exposed.[6]

When the vellox was caught, it recognized Shyvana as "the Chained One", a dragon who was merely a pet for the Demacians. Shyvana believed the vellox had no right to taunt her right after it had just murdered a man, but she acknowledged that part of her was similar to the vellox, as she enjoyed hunting her prey. After she had killed the vellox, she stopped herself from eating it, feeling disturbed by her own inhuman hunger and by the creature's terrified, humanlike face. She then hid the vellox in the darkness beneath a rock before leaving the forest.[6]

Ruination

[edit | edit source]
Main article: Rise of the Sentinels

When Viego's Harrowing reached Demacia, the Sentinels of Light and Vayne searched the Mageseekers headquarters for a fetter of Isolde's soul. There, they encountered Shyvana. After the mistreatment she had endured despite her service to the kingdom, she began to succumb to Viego's corruption. Vayne antagonized her for her magical heritage, pushing her further into Viego's service. Shyvana then collapsed the area around the Sentinels before fleeing.

After Viego acquired the fetter, the Sentinels pursued him, but were stopped by a fully ruined Shyvana. She transformed into a corrupted dragon and attacked the Sentinels while Viego escaped. After a grueling fight, the Sentinels managed to subdue her.[7] Despite Vayne's wishes to put her down, Senna insisted they spare her, and she was imprisoned within the Mageseeker compound.

Eventually, Shyvana escaped her confinement and joined Viego on the Shadow Isles as he prepared to restore his queen. She fought alongside the Ruined King in the final battle against the Sentinels, but was freed from his control after he was defeated by Gwen and the Sentinels.[8]

Great Mage Rebellion

[edit | edit source]

The Mageseeker

[edit | edit source]
Main article: The Mageseeker

After the death of King Jarvan III, the kingdom of Demacia entered a period of civil war in which the mages fought in open rebellion against the Demacian government. As the new ruler, Jarvan IV swore to avenge his father's death by by ridding Demacia of "the scourge of magic," granting increased powers to the Mageseekers and hunting down Sylas of Dregbourne, the escaped rebel who was believed to have murdered the king.[9]

Shortly after the fall of Capelworth to the rebels, Shyvana listened in on a conversation between Jarvan and Sword-Captain Garen Crownguard, who had been hunting Sylas for some time. Garen believed that the rebellion would crumble once Sylas was defeated, but Shyvana believed his confidence to be foolish—making a demon of the man would bring no peace to Demacia, nor to Jarvan himself. Shyvana told Jarvan as much once Garen had left, and also scolded Jarvan for giving the mageseekers too much power. Jarvan tried to reassure her by saying he trusted the mageseekers not to hurt her, and that no matter what happened, she would always have a place at his side. Shyvana found little comfort in his words, stating that the kind of king Jarvan was becoming would not look good with a dragon standing beside him.[10]

After Sylas returned to the Great City and assassinated Eldred Crownguard, Jarvan IV confronted Sylas on the roof, and seeing them from afar, Shyvana flew to Jarvan's aid to fulfill her duty as his bodyguard. Sylas, recognizing her as a magical being, offered for her to join his side. The half-dragon refused, simply furious that Sylas was trying to hurt Jarvan. Shyvana first fought Sylas in her humanoid form before eventually transforming into a dragon. After Sylas had bested her, Jarvan placed himself between the two of them to protect her. Sylas finally realized that Jarvan was in love with her and denounced him as a hypocrite for outlawing magic, but before he could land the killing blow on them both, Shyvana used the last of her strength to shield them with a barrier of flames, and Sylas was forced to retreat with the other rebels. Shyvana collapsed soon afterward, and Jarvan caught her before she hit the ground.[10]

Following the attack, Jarvan met with Commander Wisteria, the new Mageseeker leader after Eldred's death. Fearing he had been too lenient, Jarvan took Wisteria's advice and signed a new law which would execute any mage deemed a threat to the kingdom—an extreme measure to eliminate any chance of them being recruited by the rebels. After the decree had been signed, Shyvana argued with Jarvan on the rooftops, stating that his decision had ensured she would never have a future in Demacia. Jarvan had written his decree so that it would not apply to Shyvana, but she argued that the moment Jarvan was not around, the mageseekers would kill her, and she thus had no choice but to leave. Jarvan pleaded for her to stay, stating that he never wanted to be king and would be unable to rule without her. Shyvana offered that they could run away together to the mountains where they had lived once before. Jarvan refused, stating that he could not abandon his duty to Demacia and that it was too late for him to change. With a sad smile, Shyvana replied that "no transformation is forever" before transforming into a dragon and flying off into the distance.[10]

Later, when Wisteria began her invasion of Terbisia, Shyvana flew to provide aerial support to Sylas, Lux, and Garen, who had been sent by Jarvan to finally stop the mageseekers once and for all. After Wisteria had been defeated and Terbisia began to rebuild, Jarvan IV penned a decree that formally ended the mageseekers' operations in Demacia, challenging any noble who continued to support them to meet him on the battlefield. Jarvan also appointed Lux the governor of Terbisia, which was to be a refuge for Demacia's mages until he could bring them safety in the kingdom proper. Privately, Jarvan hoped this decree would help Shyvana to feel safe again to return to Demacia.[10]

After Jarvan disbanded the Mageseekers, Shyvana was seen returning from the horizon, finally free to come home.[10]

Appearance

[edit | edit source]

In her humanoid form, Shyvana is a tall, muscular woman with scaly purple skin, pointed ears, glowing orange eyes, and long, fiery orange hair. She maintains several draconic features in this form, including dark claws on her hands and feet, a long red tail covered in spines, dark spikes protruding from her shoulders, and long red horns on the top of her head which resemble a headdress. Before she met Jarvan IV, Shyvana wore dark brown and red leather armor with golden ornaments around the collar. As part of Jarvan's elite guard, she received new armor made from Demacian steel, which was silver and gold spiked plating across the length of her right arm and torso, with the crest of the Lightshield royal family placed in the center as a symbol of her loyalty to the crown.

In her draconic form, Shyvana becomes a massive, four-legged, fire-breathing dragon with dark red and black scales, black and gold spines, and two large red and black wings on her back. As a dragon, Shyvana maintains the same red horns, orange hair, eyes, and tail as her humanoid form.

Personality

[edit | edit source]

Despite being half dragon, Shyvana possesses human emotions because she was raised among them. She is loyal, kind, and helpful to all who show her affection, but she is not without a rather fiery temperament, when provoked.

Abilities

[edit | edit source]
  • Unique Dragon Physiology: Shyvana was born from a dragon's egg, and hatched in a humanoid form. The reason for this is unknown. Her mysterious nature granted her many powers.
    • Draconic Transformation: Shyvana can change shape from her humanoid form to her draconic form at will.
    • Superhuman Strength: Shyvana is much stronger than a normal human and is able to defeat foes far larger than her. In her dragon form, she was able to defeat her mother, a legendary dragon named Yvva, at the Battle of Wrenwall.
    • Superhuman Durability: Shyvana's durability far exceeds that of the average human, allowing her to withstand attacks that would kill other Demacians.
    • Flight: In her dragon form, Shyvana gains a pair of wings that allow her to fly at high speeds.
    • Fire Magic: In her human form, Shyvana can channel fire to embolden her physical attacks, or to send fireballs at her enemies. In her dragon form, her very breath can unleash a powerful inferno, scorching all who would oppose her.

Relations

[edit | edit source]

Jarvan IV

[edit | edit source]

Jarvan IV was saved by Shyvana after escaping from the Noxian capture and later helped her in slaying her mother, Yvva, and, out of gratitude, she earned a spot in the Demacian Elite Guard. In the Lux comics, it was revealed Jarvan loved someone that wasn't so different from the mages. This was revealed to be Shyvana in The Mageseeker.

Dragonguard

[edit | edit source]

She would later join the Dragonguard, a special branch of the Demacian military tasked in defending Demacian lands from dragons. While some are close to Shyvana and trust her, others are skeptical due to her nature.

Mageseekers

[edit | edit source]

In Rise of the Sentinels, Shyvana becomes disillusioned with Demacia's magical prejudices, she gives in to the ruination after Viego's awakening, destroying a Mageseeker compound and terrorizing the Demacian army.

Sentinels of Light

[edit | edit source]

Vayne claims that Shyvana is a monster, so she distrusted her even before she was possessed. After Lucian and Senna arrived and helped Vayne knock Ruined Shyvana out, Vayne was prepared to kill her until Senna intervened. Senna determined that Shyvana's true self is still within her. After Viego was defeated, Shyvana was freed along with the other champions from his control and returned to her normal self.

Trivia

[edit | edit source]
Shyvana, the Half-Dragon
  • In her human form, Shyvana's canonical height is 7 feet or 213.36 centimeters.[11]
  • In the Lux comics, it was stated that Jarvan IV loved someone "not so different" from mages. This person was highly speculated and later revealed to be Shyvana in The Mageseeker, where Sylas mocked Jarvan's feelings for her as "hypocritical."
  • The skull of Shyvana's mother, Yvva, is kept on display in Jarvan IV's office.[10]
Original Shyvana
  • In the  old lore, Shyvana was inducted into Demacia's elite guard after partnering with Prince Jarvan IV to slay her father's murderer. She is seen with a mixture of respect and fear amongst the people.
    • Shyvana gets along best with Xin Zhao (after Jarvan IV) while Garen disapproves of her lack of discipline and self-control.
    • Shyvana is prone to fits of rage, with Galio being the one (if not the only) capable of calming her.

    Passive: Shyvana's basic attacks on-hit will reduce Emberstrike's  current cooldown, and deal bonus magic damage based on the target's maximum health.
    Active: Each cast empowers Shyvana's next basic attack within a few seconds to trigger an additional exclusive effect. Emberstrike can be recast shortly after the attack.
    First Cast: Shyvana's slashes her claws, blasting a cone that deals physical damage to enemies hit.
    Second Cast: Shyvana slams her tail, dealing the same damage to the target and surrounding enemies.
    Dragon Form Bonus — Third Cast: Shyvana bites ferociously, dealing true damage.
