Decisive in judgment and lethal in combat, Katarina is a Noxian assassin of the highest caliber. Eldest daughter to the legendary General Du Couteau, she made her talents known with swift kills against unsuspecting enemies. Her fiery ambition has driven her to pursue heavily-guarded targets, even at the risk of endangering her allies—but no matter the mission, Katarina will not hesitate to execute her duty amid a whirlwind of serrated daggers.

"Never question my loyalty. You will never know what I endure for it." — Katarina

Background

[edit | edit source]

Early life

[edit | edit source]

Katarina Du Couteau was born to one of the most respected families in Noxus. While her younger sister Cassiopeia took after their politically brilliant mother, Katarina studied the way of the blade under her father, the empire's greatest assassin. But the wily General Marcus was a harsh teacher with many pupils; kinship held no bearing upon his high expectations. Katarina's childhood had little room for rest or kindness, every waking moment was spent honing herself into the ultimate weapon. She stole poisons from the city's apothecaries to gradually build her tolerance, and scaled the tallest towers unseen in the dead of night. For these efforts, Katarina yearned for one day to demonstrate her strength in service to the empire.[1]

To celebrate Marcus' heroism on the Shuriman front, the Du Couteaus visited the coastal city of Urzeris. But suddenly, his wife collapsed from an unknown assassin's poison. The general quickly dismissed all of their household staff, leaving the family alone in the empty house. By the time Noxus soon began invading Ionia, Marcus returned to the homeland with Katarina, while Cassiopeia remained with their mother in Shurima.[2]

Marcus finally put his daughter to the test as they joined the warbands on the western front. Her first mission was to assassinate an enemy line officer known as Demetrius. Seeing the mark as a waste of her talents, Katarina decided to instead ensure a swift victory by eliminating the enemy commander. This proved to be a terrible judgment; Demetrius took command and led a brazen charge against her father's encampment. Dozens of Noxian soldiers were slaughtered, including her father's retinue, and General Du Couteau barely made it out alive.[1]

He was furious beyond words. "The greatest assassins do not seek recognition or glory," he reminded her. "They do not expect to occupy a place of honor at their master's right hand." Overwhelmed by her shame and foolishness, Katarina fled back into the woods hoping to complete her original mission. But her distracted thoughts kept her from noticing the assassin after her until he had nearly taken her eye. Unbeknownst to her, Marcus sent after his other protégéa and adoptive son as punishment. The two continued fighting shortly after, but the man had relented; he would accept his own failure to strike true, and allow Katarina to atone for her mistake. She knew all she needed about the man, but asked of his name before leaving: Talon.[3]

Six hours later, Katarina presented Demetrius's severed head to the General. She considered taking her father's as well for sending Talon, but begrudgingly acknowledged that he had right to sentence death. Humbled by the consequences, Katarina knew she had lost her father's favor, but hoped to someday redeem herself in both his eyes and the empire.[1]

When Grand General Boram Darkwill was deposed by Jericho Swain, the Du Couteaus were among the the noble houses that honored his coup.[2]

Infiltrating the Silken Danse

[edit | edit source]
Main article: Welcome to Noxus

Some time after Noxus ended its invasion of Ionia,[4] Katarina was sent an anonymous letter from Vladimir with an invitation to the Silken Danse, and a mark to assassinate.[5][6] Undercover as a noblesse, Katarina infiltrated the manor of House Kythera and located her target: a man whom LeBlanc intended to use as the host for the Darkin Glaive. However, he was unmoved as her strike was thwarted by Elise. As the two fought across the ballroom, Katarina was able to outmaneuver Elise and quickly eliminate her mark.[7]

Working for Swain

[edit | edit source]
Main article: Katarina Comic

Four years after the coup, Marcus had mysteriously disappeared and was presumed dead. In the three years since, Katarina became the de facto head of the Du Couteau family. Despite Talon's insistence on her own merit, she could not help to feel forever incomparable to her father's legacy. Though her parents never truly accepted her, she felt appreciated by the many assignments under the new regime. Where Talon questions the new Grand General's unnatural rise to power, Katarina quickly retorts that the newly implemented Trifarix council is a sharp departure from the line of tyrants.[8]

Though the empire had begun to grow beyond its brutality, rogue warbands still sought to drag Noxus back to its past. One such warband was led by General Rache to raze the town of Stelkenmoor. As she picked off his troops, Rache begged for his life, claiming loyalty to her father and the "true" Noxus. Katarina struck him down and said that her father helped build this new Noxus; Additionally, he had failed to kill off Stelkenmoor and that his own house had disowned him, and with his death his entire family and military record would be expunged.

On another mission, Katarina was to assassinate Warmason Khogan within his estate. Once a renowned commander, Khogan had aged into reckless discretion and cost many soldiers abroad. As she broke into his house, Katarina accidentally triggered a silent door trap. Fortunately, she had a key from Piltover to disarm it, satisfied that the empire no longer shied away from tools of other nations. When Katarina confronted Khogan, she had a theory that he merely sought to die fighting a worthy opponent. She decides to honor this wish by challenging him to a duel. Short as it was, she struck him down upon his own sword and thanked him for his service, promising his rememberance as a hero.

At the Shelter Tea House, Katarina recounts the tale to Talon as a demonstration of her code of honor. Khogan deserved to die knowing that he had given his life fighting for the empire he believed in; as she would in her own death. Talon argued that this discretion is a complete disregard for their father's training, that it would have been quicker and more merciful to simply kill Khogan. "I don't know if you're going to die trying to prove father wrong… or die trying to honor him." Refusing to watch Katarina continue to risk her life so carelessly, Talon departed hoping she would eventually come to her senses.

Drann the tea shop owner brings Katarina a fresh pot of tea, when suddenly a violent explosion fills the room in smoke. As she fought back, Katarina recognized the voice of the assailant before being knocked unconscious.[8]

Assassinating Jarvan III

[edit | edit source]

When Katarina awoke, she found herself aboard a ship face to face with her father.[9] She was furious that he had faked his death, but Marcus revealed it was a cover story that allowed him to lead a covert unit into Demacia in hopes to assassinate King Jarvan III. However, he discovered that Grand General Swain's power traced back to a deal with a demon, and it was only a matter of time that this would jeopardize his leadership. He was pressed to reveal himself after receiving a written order with catastrophic changes to the mission—such as replacing Marcus with Katarina as the chosen assassin. Marcus believed that Katarina's recent successes were a ploy to elevate her, only to send her on a practical suicide mission. Such a failure would ruin their reputation and provide impetus for Swain to wage war against the noble houses, consummating absolute power in Noxus.

It was troubling for Katarina to take her father's word of these incredible charges so suddenly. Torn between loyalties, these doubts stirred in her mind all the way to the City of Demacia, where the king is expected to attend a public execution. Disguised as a peasant, she explored the palace grounds and determined that the prescribed kill from range would not suffice against the royal security; instead she would have to approach her target hard and fast from the crowd. In the meantime, she decided to pursue a secondary mark in the assignment: a patrician named Lux Crownguard whose secret magic powers might become a future problem for Noxus. Katarina approached the estate just as the girl was blasting her way out. She was quickly cornered by guardsmen, but one Captain Arrika argued against outright killing the young mage. Katarina quickly dispatched the rest of the guards, and claimed that she was a secret agent named Dereen sent by Garen to retrieve Lux. Arrika leaves satisfied, but Lux could immediately see right through the bluff.

Katarina pressed Lux about what she was trying to find, and the girl admitted it was to save her friend Sylas from execution. He and all mages are outlawed and persecuted in Demacia. Despite being able to hide within high-profile nobility, Lux decided to advocate against this treatment of whom are otherwise good Demacian people; a position that goes against official doctrine. Katarina was moved by such character—one very much unlike what she already heard about Demacians, yet sharing a devotion to change the nation's ideals for the better. Believing she could serve as a useful distraction, Katarina chose to set her free. As Lux hurried off, she informed that King Jarvan opted out of attending the event amid civil unrest, and would be kept safe in the palace.[9]

Word followed of Sylas's escape from his execution, and the king was evacuated to a secret bunker within the inner chambers. As he sat easy alone in the room, Katarina emerged and struck behind him. As he lay dying, she taunted him for being a ruler who brutalized those who are stronger than himself. "All Demacians will realize their true strength… under the banner of Noxus." She then left the chamber, cutting down the king's guards as she escaped.[10]

As Katarina made her way along the rooftops, she was intercepted by Garen Crownguard accusing her as an accomplice in the botched execution. As she remained tenacious against his surprising speed, she mentioned saving Lux from the Crownguard's own soldiers. Garen insisted he still had to take her into custody, but fight was interrupted by an explosion in the streets, and both realized they had no time to continue. Garen rushed off to find his sister, while Katarina resumed her escape.

Riots in the streets prompted the city gates to close shut, so Katarina took a Silverwing rider hostage to make her escape over the wall. However, the rider was shot by Captain Jaredan, causing Katarina to plummet into the surrounding forest.[10] As she laid unconscious, Katarina dreamt about saving her father and Talon from Zed. However, her dream turned into a nightmare as her brother and father morphed into demonic ravens, waking Katarina from her slumber. Miraculously, she landed on a giant slug that broke her fall, but one of her daggers had shattered.[11]

Confronting Family

[edit | edit source]

Two weeks later, Katarina returned to Noxus and took shelter in Drann's tea shop. Knowing that her would-be assassin Jaredan had followed her, Katarina took his presence as confirmation that a conspiracy was unfolding against her family. Drann offered to craft her a new weapon, but she declined. Katarina intended to meet with Talon next to ensure he was safe, but was prepared in case he was involved.

Katarina returned home later that night, and Talon awoke from bed to greet her. She firmly asked when he had last seen their father, and Talon replied that it had been before Marcus' death. As Katarina drew closer, her suspicion caught the scent of a familiar fragrance on him. She then attacked him, demanding to know how long he had been hiding the truth. Talon was quick to disarm her weapon, and revealed that he had helped Marcus fake his death. Raising the dagger against her, Talon declared that he would replace her as the heir and stand beside Marcus when he kills Swain.

Katarina quickly drew her broken dagger to break free, inflicting the same wound to his eye that he had given her many years ago. As Talon lay wounded, Katarina told him that he had become what she once was: a child desperate to earn their father's approval. She knew Talon would not believe her, convinced instead that he was acting out of loyalty to Noxus. Katarina had learned better than to trust their father, they had always been nothing more than tools to him. As she prepared to leave, Talon lashed out, accusing her as one of Swain's traitors. Katarina hoped that Talon would some day grow beyond what Marcus made him, but warned that she would come for him if he didn't.

Katarina returned to Drann's tea shop and lamented on what her broken family said about her. Drann reassured that it meant she was still good in a world of monsters, and urged rest from her wounds. The next morning, Drann offered to accompany Katarina as she prepared to confront her father, but she tearfully declined, insisting it was family business. Together, they then prepare a ritual to empower a new set of daggers with Drann's hemomancy.[11] Drann drew Katarina's blood and began binding the daggers to it, suggesting Katarina to clear her mind. She needed to focus on a moment when she had felt truly connected to something—by warm instinct rather than cold logic. She recalled her duel with Garen, and the fleeting amicable moment before parting ways. When the ritual was complete, the daggers were blasted away. At Drann's instruction, Katarina reached out to them. One dagger now answered her will, magically returning to her hand, while the other allowed her to blink to its position. Knowing that Talon would have informed their father by now, Katarina set out to protect the Grand General. Drann questioned whether it was necessary, noting that Swain could easily sense danger. Katarina was certain that if Marcus was moving against Swain, he had already devised a way to kill him.[12]

Katarina was already waiting before he arrived. She confronted him over his betrayal in Demacia, and Marcus revealed that while the mission was genuine, Lux had never been the secondary target; he was. Cutting the pleasantries short, he hurled a smoke grenade at Katarina and attacked from the shadows. She found quickly that she stood no chance to fight with the same techniques he taught her, so she chose to fight as the Noxian she had become. She taunted Marcus, claiming that she found Talon again and turned him over to be tortured by Swain. This bold-faced lie enraged only a little more than an angry footstep, but the sound was enough mistake for her to strike first. As Katarina pressed the attack, she scolded Marcus' tutelage. "A true Noxian wants their child to grow up stronger than them." He taught her everything she knows, but could never bear to witness his daughter surpassing himself. Katarina could only beat him now once she stopped trying to outperform the master, and attained the ingenuity to learn new skills.

As Marcus sat defeated, Katarina held back to declare his arrest. He retorted by priming a devastating Wolf's Maw explosive, intending to kill Swain along with the entire fortress as collateral. Katarina used her daggers to seize the device, but with no time to disarm it, she prepared to hurl herself far enough away to save Noxus in glorious death. Before she could act, Swain intervened on wings above, wrenching the charge from her grasp and channeling it to kill Marcus in a single blast.

In the aftermath, Swain apologized for arriving late. Marcus' magic had shrouded him from even Swain's demonic sight, but fortunately he had been able to sense the younger Du Couteau. Katarina remarked at his monstrous power, and the Grand General admitted that he was no longer the same man he once was. He thanked her for her service and said she had earned his trust. Even after reforming the nation's politics, Swain needed an assassin that could reach even himself, should his powers compromise him. Katarina assured Swain that she knew exactly how to kill him.

Two months later, Swain named Katarina as the head of the Du Couteau assassins guild, while Talon retreated to Shurima. She made her way to the northern Freljord and assassinated Jaredan, declaring that the empire's fate was now her's to decide.[12]

Appearance

[edit | edit source]

Katarina is portrayed as a slender woman with peach skin and an athletic feminine physique. Her most prominent features include her long bright red hair and a scar on her left eye.

She usually wears a revealing assassin uniform made of black and gray leather and latex which reveals her abdomen and several dark blue tattoos, she wears leather heeled boots and a small leather jacket.

Personality

[edit | edit source]

As heir to a house of assassins, Katarina had pursued her father's approval for many years, but always remained short of his standards. Having lost the respect of her parents, Katarina developed her own ideals as a Noxian and is deeply loyal to the empire. Rather than chase legacies, she would use her skills to make a Noxus that she believes in. She is a firm believer in individual merit, and recognizes the benefit of having allies, especially those of different skillsets. Therefore, she finds mutual appreciation under the Trifarix.

Katarina embraces the philsophies of the new Noxus under Swain, believing the empire is stronger for growing beyond its brutality, and holds disdain for the conservative factions.[8]

As an assassin, Katarina is an agile fighter, can quickly plan her strategies, and has a keen eye for the subtlest of clues. Behind these instincts, her personal code of conduct dictates she would kill targets based on how honorable she regards them, also shows a somewhat flirtatious personality with Garen.

Abilities

[edit | edit source]
  • Agile Combat Mastery: Katarina is both agile and quick, capable of performing acrobatic maneuvers in the midst of battle and proving difficult to seize when the fight seems over.
  • Stealth Mastery: As an assassin, Katarina is trained to infiltrate heavy security in order to approach her target. Her stealth is comprised of part silence and part elusiveness.
  • Knife Mastery: Katarina's ambidexterity makes excellent use of twin daggers.
    • Blood Bound Blades: Katarina's blood bond with her blades allows her to retrieve them telekinetically or reappear next to them at the blink of an eye. She possesses twin daggers infused with her blood by a hemomancy ritual conducted by Drann as a gift to replace the old daggers.
    • Daggers of Du Couteau: Katarina inherited twin daggers for her assassin profession. One shattered during her assignment in Demacia, and she left the other with Talon.

Relations

[edit | edit source]

Talon Du Couteau

[edit | edit source]

In contrast to Cassiopeia, Talon is the adopted son of Marcus Du Couteau. In youth, Talon was tasked with a mission by Katarina's father: to eliminate her. However, Katarina miraculously survived Talon's attempt, emerging from the encounter with a distinct scar across her left eye. This encounter created an unexpected bond between them, as both had failed in their respective missions.

After their encounter, he adopted a nonchalant demeanor towards Katarina, assuming the role of her composed younger brother. Behind this facade, however, he secretly conspired with his adopted father to overthrow Jericho Swain, who had recently ascended to power. Talon nursed a deep-seated jealousy towards Katarina, who had been entrusted with the symbolic daggers of House Du Couteau, a clear indication of the rightful heir.

Upon revealing his intentions, Katarina responded with unexpected empathy. She understood that Talon was merely a puppet, obediently carrying out her father's orders without question. The failed assassination attempt, rather than fostering animosity, prompted Katarina to reflect on her family dynamics. As she departed from Talon, she wished for him to break free from her father's control and forge his own destiny. However, with a resolute warning, she made it clear that if Talon remained shackled by her father's influence, she would be compelled to bring an end to him herself.

Cassiopeia Du Couteau

[edit | edit source]

Katarina interacts very little with her younger sister, Cassiopeia, since both live in different worlds. While Katarina was taught by her father, pushing her to learn the way of the blade, Cassiopeia took after their politically brilliant mother.

Marcus Du Couteau

[edit | edit source]

Katarina and her father, Marcus Du Couteau, had a very strained relationship. According to Marcus, her daughter always seemed to fail him as an assassin. To him, she was to brash and killed with a sense of passion and not with a cold, mercilessness he believed was necessary for an assassin. Because of this he ordered a young Talon to kill his firstborn daughter. After multiple attempts on her life failed, she confronted her father. She told the general she had considered taking his head instead, but eventually decided—as much as she hated to admit it—that he had done the right thing in ordering her death. She had failed. Not just as an assassin, or as a daughter, but as a Noxian. She knew she had lost her father’s favor, and could never regain it. He would raise others in her place, simply to spite her. Still, she vowed to redeem herself, no matter the cost—to rededicate her talents to the empire, and to become the sinister weapon she always intended to be.

After her father mysteriously disappeared in 993 AN, she starts taking orders from Swain. Three years later, he reveals himself to her, saying that Swain is going to send her on a mission to Demacia to die. After not only completing her mission successfully but coming out of it alive, she returns to Noxus. Realising that her father is trying to kill Swain, she immediatly moved to stop him.

During their final confrontation, she says to her father that he always wanted her to fail, because he was afraid she would grow to be better then him. As their battle came to a climax, Marcus tried to take both Katarina and Swain with him by using an explosive known as the "Wolf's Maw". Before it could detonate, Swain arrived to redirect the explosion to Marcus, instantly incinerating him.

Soreana Du Couteau

[edit | edit source]

Katarina interacts very little with her mother, Soreana Du Couteau, since both live in different worlds. While Katarina was taught by her father, pushing her to learn the way of the blade, her mother focused her attention on to her younger sister, Cassiopeia.

Garen Crownguard

[edit | edit source]

Encountering each other in the midst of the chaos during Sylas' Mage Rebellion in Demacia, Garen, acting on misinformation, confronts Katarina under the belief that she is aiding Sylas. After mentioning she saved his sister from capture, she suggested Garen should do the same. After some fierce clashes of steel, an explosion stops their battle. Both combantants agree that they don't have time for their scuffle. Garen and Katarina have a small moment, bonding over their shared grievances with their younger siblings. Before she leaves, Katarina says to Garen she sees a great future for Garen and his sister.

While it is unclear that Garen has any interest in Katarina, Katarina has some interest in him. During her bonding ritual with her weapons, she focused on a moment when she was not trying to figure out every angle, where she just felt something and connected. The moment she chose was her first encounter with Garen, smiling faintly.

Luxanna Crownguard

[edit | edit source]

Assigned the mission to assassinate Lux and King Jarvan III in Demacia, Katarina deviates from her orders after recognizing Lux's dissent from the corrupt Demacian hierarchy. Sympathizing with Lux's plight, seeing as both women had been rejected by their families. Katarina spares her life. Katarina also notes that she thinks Lux is a threat to the Demacian hierarchy and would actually make a good Noxian.

Jericho Swain

[edit | edit source]

Initially aligning herself with Swain after the coup that toppled Grand General Boram Darkwill, Katarina becomes one of Swain's most devoted assassins, eliminating political rivals and enforcing the new order. Despite a moment of mistrust when she learns of her father's faked death and Swain's alleged betrayal, Katarina chooses to continue working for Swain to secure her family's safety. During Katarina's battle against her father, Marcus Du Couteau, Swain, recognizing Katarina as his failsafe, intervenes to prevent her sacrifice, cementing her role as his trusted assassin.

Drann Hagarr

[edit | edit source]

The proprietor of a teahouse where Katarina seeks refuge and counsel, Drann, is a close friend and ally of Katarina's. She bound Katarina to her weapons with blood magic. This bond allows Katarina to teleport to her weapons. Drann is a stabilizing force in Katarina's otherwise painful and complicated life. She offers Katarina guidance, a sense of understanding and provides her with a source of solace.

Trivia

[edit | edit source]
  • It is no secret that Katarina and Garen are in a relationship; almost everyone knows about them and their "secret".
    • To name some: Jarvan does not get what he sees on her, Tiana Crownguard (Garen's aunt) does not approve, Maokai has stated to stop carving on his back because "Everyone knows G <3 K" and Lux hopes they are just holding hands or she is telling.
      • Though many of these may be hypothetical cases.
    • There was going to be a cinematic that had Garen and Katarina working together while he had her as a prisoner; a possible alternate way of them meeting. This was changed later with the release of numerous comics that illustrated the moments where they met.
    • Their relationship may be a parallel to the classic novel Romeo and Juliet, as they are lovers from two factions that are confronted between them.
  • Out of the two biological Du Couteau sisters, she is the only one we have seen the hair from, as Cassiopeia always has her hair covered.
